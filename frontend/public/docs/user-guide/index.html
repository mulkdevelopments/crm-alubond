Getting started

Alubond CRM helps your team track construction projects, log field activity, manage follow-ups, and monitor pipeline performance — from the office or on site.

What you need
  • A company email address and password for Alubond CRM (provided when your account is set up).
  • A modern web browser: Chrome, Safari, or Edge — on desktop, tablet, or phone.
  • Location permission when logging site visits (GPS is recorded automatically).
Open the CRM
  • Sign in at crm.alubond.com using the email and password for your account.
  • Bookmark this link on your phone or laptop for quick access in the field.
  • Do not have an account yet? Use Request access on the login page — your request is reviewed by the team.

Your first day — quick path

  1. Sign in and review the Dashboard for your pipeline summary.
  2. Open Pipeline and find projects assigned to you.
  3. Tap a project to open its detail page and log your first activity (call, visit, or note).
  4. Check Follow-ups for any tasks due today or overdue.

Sign in

Open the login page and enter the email and password provided for your account.

  1. Go to crm.alubond.com.
  2. Enter your email and password.
  3. Click Sign in. You are taken to the Dashboard.

No account or forgot password?

  • Request access — on the login page, submit your name and email if you are new to the CRM.
  • Forgot password — enter your email to receive a reset link (check spam if it does not arrive within a few minutes).
Session tips
  • Your session stays active for several days; sign out from Profile when using a shared device.
  • If sign-in fails, double-check your email and password, or use Forgot password on the login page.
  • Still stuck? Contact your manager or company support with your email address and a screenshot of the error.

Dashboard

Your home screen summarises pipeline health, targets, and the latest field activity for projects you can see.

What you’ll see

  • KPI cards — open deals, pipeline value, won/lost counts (scoped to your role).
  • Pipeline funnel — projects grouped by stage.
  • Recent activity — latest notes, calls, and visits logged on projects.
  • Follow-up summary — quick link to overdue and due-today tasks.

Pipeline

The pipeline is where you browse and work on projects. Each card shows project name, location, stage, value, and assigned owner.

Browse projects

  • Use the search box to filter by project name, city, or developer.
  • On desktop, projects appear in kanban columns by stage; drag a card to move stage (managers+).
  • On mobile, swipe between stage tabs or use the stage picker.
  • Click any project card to open its detail page.

Add or edit a project (managers, regional managers, CEO)

  1. Click Add project (or the edit icon on an existing card).
  2. Fill in project name, city, country, developer, value, and location on the map.
  3. Assign regional manager, manager, and sales reps as needed.
  4. Save — the project appears in the pipeline for the assigned team.

When moving a project to Tender or later stages, you may be asked for commercial details (value, quantity, and product specifications).

Sales reps typically view assigned projects and log activity; they do not create new projects unless your process assigns that to managers.

Project detail & field activity

The project page is the main workspace for day-to-day selling — timeline, stakeholders, and logging visits.

Log an update

Open the activity section on the project page and click to add an update. Supported types:

TypeWhen to use
NoteGeneral update or internal comment
CallPhone conversation with a contact
VisitOn-site meeting — GPS location is captured automatically
EmailEmail sent or received
WhatsAppWhatsApp message exchange

Logging a site visit

  1. Select activity type Visit.
  2. Enter who you met and the meeting date/time.
  3. Allow location access when prompted — your GPS coordinates are saved with the visit.
  4. Add a recap of what was discussed and any next steps.
  5. Optionally attach photos or a voice note, then click Log update.
Visits also send a location ping to the system, which helps managers see field coverage on the team view.

Stakeholders

Scroll to the stakeholders section to see architects, consultants, contractors, and other contacts linked to the project. Use Add stakeholder to record name, role, organisation, email, and phone.

Attachments

When logging activity you can attach files (PDFs, images) or record a voice note. Attachments appear on the activity timeline and can be played or downloaded from there.

Follow-ups

Follow-ups are action items tied to projects — calls to make, emails to send, or meetings to schedule.

Working your task list

  1. Open Follow-ups from the sidebar or dashboard link.
  2. Review Overdue and Due today sections first.
  3. Click a task to see project context and contact details.
  4. After completing the action, mark the task Done.
  5. Done tasks move to the completed section for reference.
Follow-ups can be created when logging project activity (set a due date on the activity form) or by your manager as part of pipeline reviews.

Geo Intel (Map)

The map shows project pins for everything in your scope. Use it to plan routes and see geographic coverage.

  • On desktop, pan and zoom the map freely.
  • On phone, tap Tap to use map before panning; tap Lock map when you want to scroll the page again.
  • Click a pin to see project name, stage, and city.
  • Use stage filter chips to show only Lead, Tender, Won, etc.
  • Open the project detail page from a marker popup.

Field Team

Available to managers, regional managers, and leadership — a hierarchy view of your team’s targets, pipeline, visits, and recent activity.

How to use it

  • Start at your level in the org chart and expand cards to see people below you.
  • Each person shows target vs achieved, pipeline value, visit counts, and recent visits.
  • Click a rep’s recent visit to jump to the project activity.
  • Use this view in weekly reviews to spot reps who need support or celebrate wins.
Sales reps do not see the Field Team menu — they use Dashboard and Pipeline for their own assigned work.

Profile & account

Manage your display name, password, and session from the Profile page.

Update your name

  1. Open Profile from the sidebar (desktop) or bottom nav (mobile).
  2. Edit First name and Last name.
  3. Click Save profile.

Change password

  1. Enter your current password.
  2. Enter and confirm your new password.
  3. Click Update password.

Sign out

Click Sign out at the bottom of the Profile page, especially on shared or public devices.
